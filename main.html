SMARTPHONE POSITION TRACKING USING GNSS

Smartphones are increasingly being used for most applications, including cadastral surveying, mapping surveying applications, and navigation. The goal of this project is to compute smartphone location, which could enable services that require lane-level accuracy, such as high-occupancy vehicle services, and to bridge the gap between geospatial information of finer human behaviours and mobile internet with improved granularity.



Why GNSS and not GPS?

GNSS and GPS work in tandem, but the main difference between the two is that GNSS-compatible equipment can use navigational satellites from networks other than the GPS system, and more satellites means greater receiver accuracy and reliability.
